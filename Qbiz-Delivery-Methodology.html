Overview

The purpose of this methodology is to provide tips and tricks crowd-sourced from Qbiz’ very best consultants, that should help put you on the path to success for any type of client project

Before interviewing with a client

Depending on how fast we move with a client, you might not have the time to cycle through these steps prior to meeting w/ the client. Do the best you can with the time allotted. 

Also, if you feel that there is a mismatch between your skills and what will be required for a project - could you potentially ramp up on gaps within a few days? If so, talk to your client director. We may be able to move a screen by a few days to buy you some time to prepare correctly

  • 411 on the client
    • Go to their website / install their App
    • How do they make money? Search for 10-k financial statements if applicable
    • Latest mentions in the news (key acquisitions, new products)
    • Who are their competitors?
    • Mission statement?
    • Ongoing priorities / initiatives?
    • Areas of growth? How saturated is their total addressable market?
    • Who do I know there?
    • Who are the key players you’ll interact with?
    • What's the client's tech stack?
      • If cannot find on the web, ask the sales team / your manager for these details
  • Get a clear understanding of the project
    • Talk to your manager / sales director / client team member for the details
  • Over-prepare for the tech-screen
    • Use our question bank in our Google drive
    • Talk to your colleagues for some tips and tricks - especially those who have worked with the client

When interviewing with a client

  • Be honest and transparent about your experience and exposure
    • For example, if you know nothing about docker and you know that this will come up prior to the interview
      • Don’t: Zero experience with docker
      • Do: Spend some time prior to the interview (even if its the morning of) looking at high-level literature on Docker, and identify some courses to sign up on.
      • Ping some colleagues to set up a brain dump. During the interview, say that you’ve started to ramp up on Docker, and that you’re hoping to have considerable exposure on it by the proj start date.
      • If any questions come up on Docker, punt on them and tell the interviewer that you’ll get back to them in a few days with the answer, after ramping up on it
  • Ask questions about the project (desired outcomes, day to day)
  • Find a way to give your potential areas of growth a positive spin
  • Be mindful of your speaking tempo and enunciation
  • Pause and breathe before answering questions. Use active-listening
  • Ask questions, state assumptions, and walk interviewers through your thought process / game plan before writing a single line of code
  • Keep your solutions as simple as possible
    • Get points on the board and then make your solution more elegant
  • Align your answers with their existing stacks
    • E.g. if they use Snowflake, answer SQL questions using Snowflake SQL
  • Break down a problem into manageable increments. Iterative and conversational approach
  • Write readable, production-level code
  • If there's a whiteboard, USE IT
  • Document questions + answer keys of your screen immediately after (while it’s fresh!) to support colleagues in future mtg preps
    • Under the /Clients folder on our google drive
    • Create an interviews folder if it doesn’t exist

When starting a project

  • Get in the right mailing lists and slack channels
  • Be overly assertive when self-provisioning accesses and privileges
    • Be prepared to answer why you need certain levels of access (provide project context)
    • Ask the client / colleagues for literature to ramp up while waiting for access
  • Qbiz + Client Intros
    • Include client director / engagement manager
  • Get more clarity on project specifics
    • Key deliverables
    • Stacks and Resources at your disposal
    • Data sources
    • Who are the key players in the proj?
      • Project contributors, project SMEs, potential opposers, direct/indirect stakeholders
      • Who are you directly reporting to at the client site?
      • Who approves hrs / signs the checks?
      • Who can answer questions and unblock me?
  • Complete compliance trainings as needed
  • Be on top of your jira tickets
    • Be mindful not to become overloaded w/ tasks. Push back if necessary or question whether some things can be deprioritized or delayed
  • Build a project schedule (even if its internal), scoping tasks, duration, and dependencies
  • Comms plan (Stand Ups, status reporting, progress meeting)
  • Refine list of key players you’ll liaise with
    • Who’s technical? Who has business knowledge?
  • Prep and conduct a kick off meeting
    • Send Kick-off deck to client after kick-off meeting
  • Socialize risks and blockers with stakeholders
  • Understand existing standards we need to adhere to
  • Have a shared drive for proj details + meeting notes
  • Reuse templates / scripts / tools
  • Make sure you re-emphasize any upcoming vacations to your client

During a project

  • Be proactive with status reports
    • Schedule recurring meetings (stand ups)
    • Consistent cadence of sending status reports. Use our existing template
      • Resources, hrs, accomplishments this week/nxt
      • Risks, blockers, mitigations
  • Socialize milestones and wins
  • Understand task priorities
  • Regularly course-correct the proj schedule (with stakeholder approval)
  • Leverage your consulting network
  • Get a clear understanding of what business value your work is providing
  • Resource Management and availability (Time - People - Money)
    • Availability bottlenecks, cycle time allocation, burn down rate, etc
  • Conduct impact analyses with new curveballs you encounter
  • Proactively inform Qbiz leadership of any opportunities
    • New potential projects
    • Extensions
    • Placing other consultants
  • Internal Collaboration Documents
    • Keeps track of potential opportunities based on expressed / identified client needs
      • E.g. people departing/moving teams, new needs that arise, etc
      • Project extensions
      • Scour confluence to find more of the above
      • Communicate these to our sales team proactively
    • Internal docs - Onboarding checklists, raw ideas captured, links, catch-all notes, anything that will make life easier for future Qbiz colleagues
  • Scope creep - balancing act between firm and flexible
    • Understand task priorities
    • It’s a give and take game. If new work is added, either we de-prioritize things, or the deadline moves
    • Talk to your manager if faced with conflicting priorities (e.g. supporting two diff stakeholders)
    • Be wary of folks wanting to farm their work your way
  • Educate yourself on things that the client may do better than you
  • Establish success criteria
    • What is engagement performance evaluated on?
    • Will individuals be evaluated separately?
    • Who will be signing off on deliverables?
    • How do deliverables tie into strategy?
    • What can you do to manage scope?
    • What are the dependencies?
    • What are the unknowns?
    • Identify what is Product Requirements Doc (PRD) Deliverable vs Proof of Concept (POC)
    • Over communicate if something is exploratory/POC
    • Identify how learnings from POC will be captured
    • Allot some cycle times at the end of a sprint / deliverable to plan for the next body of work
  • Got a tough problem?
    • Timebox, leverage your network (including Qbiz colleagues) if you cannot solve it within ~20m
  • When giving client presentations
    • Give kudos to those that helped you
    • Do a few dry runs. Your Qbiz lead / manager will happily support you. Give them as much lead time as possible!
    • Add graphics / cues. Leverage speaker notes!
    • Allow for questions midway through and at the end
  • Need to have a tough convo with a client?
    • Rope in manager / mentor to brainstorm
    • Communicate issue to client on a timely manner, with solution paths in mind
    • Be proactive, transparent and honest
    • Always make sure the client is in the loop

When wrapping up a project

  • Socialize your approaching project end date with stakeholders with enough lead time
    • This gives them some time to try and get you extended
    • Try to meet directly with stakeholders to highlight the value you add to the client
  • Set expectations with stakeholders on what will be delivered vs not by project close, and their % completed status
    • This pertains to Jira tickets as well that you might be working on
  • Documentation
    • Crisp documentation is an ongoing calling card
    • Put together delivery documentation
    • On recommendations made - <recommendation, value proposition, LOE>
    • Additional work that needs to be done, but was either
      • Not a priority in this project
      • Couldn’t be completed b/c of time constraints
    • Put into words the business value that your project is generating for your client
      • These are irrespective of whether they were implemented during the project
  • Learn who will be taking over your work. Set up knowledge xfer sessions with them ASAP
  • Think about planting seeds for continuing work. Convince the person taking over your work that they could use your assistance.  Call out additional next steps that you would pursue if you continued working on a project.
  • Work with Qbiz client director/delivery mgr to provide scoping of work that still needs to be completed and what a next phase of project would look like
  • Complete the last status report with all relevant details
  • Make a note of everybody you would poach in the future
  • Back up all your files in the Qbiz Gdrive
  • Write a customer success story. Templates can be found here
  • Depending on the nature of your project, it may make sense to have a wrap-up / final presentation. Here’s an example
  • Write a compelling blog post!
  • Add everyone to LinkedIn
    • Stakeholders, managers, ICs, leadership, vendors you liaised with. Everyone
  • Ask stakeholders for a LinkedIn testimonial
  • Send them a testimonial request via LinkedIn
  • Once you wrap up the project, please plan on attending the following bench meeting on the following Monday @ 9AM PST

Did I win in my last project?

  • You delivered what you promised, and more
    • Work product
    • Within deadlines / budget
  • Client is complimentary of your work
  • Client got their expected value out of you
  • Client is willing to hire you again or recommend you/Qbiz
    • For a different project
    • To other companies
  • The assets you built were adopted by multiple users
  • Did you learn anything fruitful during this project?

Re: LinkedIN

  • Add everyone to LinkedIn
    • The sooner the better. Preferably after first quick win is established
  • Update your LI / consultant profile with latest project info
  • If the project end is nearing, add them BEFORE you leave, and ask relevant stakeholders for a testimonial
    • If you accidently left and did not ask. That’s fine. Add + Ask ASAP
  • Very few clients will give you a testimonial unless you ask. So ask.
  • How do I know the client will write me a rave review?
    • If you get any warm fuzzies as per the “Did I win in my latest project” section, you should be good
  • To get a testimonial
    • Navigate to the client’s page > click on more > request a recommendation
  • Be prepared to ask the client for a recommendation more than once

Post-Project

  • Decompress
  • Build customer success story on your client, following this template
  • Work with your lead / manager to define a upskilling curriculum
    • Great to have these align with your SMART goals
    • Consider taking 2 - 3 different courses at a time
  • Schedule a post-mortem with your manager
  • Provide presales support
  • Add new merit badges to your career matrix
  • Update LinkedIn accordingly
  • Could we potentially package up your proj and resell it? Write it up
  • Best use of your down time
    • Training / certs / courses
    • Blog posts
    • Qram
    • Infrastructure improvements in the org
    • Internal / reusable tooling
    • Great time to knock out a few team clinics
  • Synch up with the sales team so you’re top of mind
