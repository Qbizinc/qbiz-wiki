Checklist for managing billing and costs on Google Cloud Platform (GCP):

1. Create a budget: Set up a budget for your GCP project to track your costs and alert you when you exceed your allocated budget.

2. Review your usage and costs: Use GCP's billing reports to track your usage and costs across various services and projects.

3. Optimize your resources: Use GCP's cost optimization tools, such as rightsizing, reservations, and recommendations, to optimize your resource usage and reduce costs.

4. Set up alerts: Configure alerts to notify you when costs exceed a certain threshold, so you can take action to control your spending.

5. Use cost allocation: Use GCP's cost allocation feature to allocate costs to different departments or teams, making it easier to track expenses.

6. Monitor your billing data: Use GCP's billing data export to export your billing data and monitor your usage and costs in real-time.

7. Review your billing statements: Review your billing statements regularly to ensure that you're being charged correctly and to identify any unexpected charges.

8. Manage access and permissions: Limit access to your GCP billing account to authorized personnel only, and review your IAM policies regularly to ensure that access is appropriate.

9. Use third-party tools: Consider using third-party tools, such as cloud cost management tools, to manage your GCP costs and optimize your spending.

 

By following this checklist, you can ensure that you're effectively managing your GCP costs and avoiding unexpected charges.
