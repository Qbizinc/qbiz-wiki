Checklist for networking virtual networks in GCP:

1. Define your network topology: Determine how you want to connect your virtual networks, and what kind of traffic you want to allow between them.  

2. Create your virtual networks: Use the GCP console or command-line interface to create your virtual networks.  

3. Configure network peering: If you want to connect two or more virtual networks, you'll need to configure network peering.  

4. Set up firewall rules: Use GCP's firewall rules to control incoming and outgoing traffic to your virtual networks.  

5. Configure VPN or interconnect: If you need to connect your virtual networks to on-premises networks or other cloud providers, you can use VPN or interconnect.  

6. Monitor network traffic: Use GCP's monitoring tools to keep an eye on network traffic and troubleshoot any issues that arise.
